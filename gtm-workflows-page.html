The Build Framework

Every workflow is built on the same four pillars.

GTM Workflows builds operating logic across People, Technology, Context, and Data — the same four pillars your Diagnosis assessed.

01 · People
People

Reps default to intuition when prioritization signals aren't built into the motion. Workflows define who contacts which accounts, when, in what order, and how managers coach to it.

→ Call Grading + Coaching Rhythm→ Change Management + Enablement→ Activity + Adoption Tracking
02 · Technology
Technology

We build on your existing stack — not ours. We map the full infrastructure, specify every configuration required, and build the integrations that make your tools compound instead of conflict.

→ Tech Stack Integration→ AI Workflow Monitoring + Governance→ Deal Desk Automation
03 · Context
Context

Generic outreach at scale is expensive noise. Every workflow that touches a buyer is built around specific context — the right message to the right person based on what's actually happening in their world.

→ Conversation Catalist Detection→ Pre-Meeting Deck Build→ Competitive Battlecard Build
04 · Data
Data

A workflow is only as strong as the data underneath it. We build the data layer first — enrichment, hygiene, lead source attribution, lifecycle tracking — so every workflow above it fires on accurate inputs.

→ Data Enrichment + Validation→ CRM Hygiene + Data Integrity→ Full-Funnel Attribution Build + Monitoring
The Execution Gap

Revenue consequences don't appear randomly.

They're the predictable output of missing infrastructure. This is what that gap looks like — and what closes it.

✕ Without workflow infrastructure
—

Reps spend hours researching accounts they'll never reach. What they find is incomplete. What they send reflects it.

—

Leads route manually and slowly. Speed-to-lead collapses. The first impression goes to whoever responds first — which isn't you.

—

Deals slip without detection. No risk signal. No trigger. You find out on the forecast call when it's already too late.

—

Churn surprises you. Health data lives in three systems that don't talk to each other. No one owns the signal.

—

Expansion is ad hoc. Revenue from existing customers depends entirely on relationship quality and timing.

✓ With workflow infrastructure
→

Catalist-based briefing surfaces the right account at the right moment. Reps arrive prepared — not scrambling.

→

Automated scoring and routing assigns leads in minutes. Speed-to-lead becomes a structural advantage, not a heroic effort.

→

Deal risk scoring flags at-risk opportunities before they're lost. Intervention happens while there's still time to act.

→

Health scoring aggregates every signal into a single view and triggers intervention before the conversation gets hard.

→

Expansion opportunities are identified from usage and engagement data. The motion runs whether or not the relationship is warm.
