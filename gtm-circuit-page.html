Definition

A GTM Circuit is what AI-native looks like in practice.

A GTM Circuit is a triggered orchestration of steps that converts a signal into a revenue outcome and feeds the outcome back into the next run. Because it loops, your context layer gets sharper, your AI generation gets more specific, and each run outperforms the last.

What it isn't
Not a Sequence

A sequence is one commercial step inside a Circuit. It's the delivery mechanism, not the loop.

Not a Play

A play is a set of instructions for a rep to follow. It doesn't execute itself and it doesn't feed back.

Not an Automation

An automation runs logic. A GTM Circuit reads context, generates from it, and loops the output back in. Different machine entirely.

GTM Grid

What powers a Circuit and where most orgs are broken.

Context, Data, Process, and Technology. Every step of the Circuit draws from one or more of them. Most orgs are breaking in at least two pillars without knowing it: reps doing research the context layer should have assembled, sequences running off data that's dirty and unstructured, tools that were never wired together. Anatomy

Every GTM Circuit follows the same anatomy.

Sequences are binary. They run off a single pillar with no guardrails, which means a great playbook collapses the moment the data feeding it is dirty, or the technology supporting it wasn't built for it, or the messaging has no context behind it. A GTM Circuit is intentionally designed across all four pillars. Because every pillar is governed and continuously updated, the Circuit doesn't just run, it evolves.
