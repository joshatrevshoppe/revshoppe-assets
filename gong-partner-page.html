The problem with most Gong rollouts

Recording calls is not the same as learning from them.

The average Gong implementation produces call recordings, some AI summaries, a handful of trackers, and a deal board that managers glance at before pipeline reviews. What it rarely produces is a closed loop. A buyer mentions a competitor. A rep objects to pricing in a way that costs the deal. A persona says "we just don't have budget right now" — in six different calls over six months. Gong captured all of it. None of it changed the ICP profile, the battle card, the outbound sequence, or what new reps learn in onboarding. That is the gap RevShoppe closes.

Common questions

What organizations ask before they engage.

We've had Gong for two years and nobody is really acting on what it surfaces. Where do we start?
With a GTM Diagnosis. In most Gong environments that have stopped producing value, the problem is one of three things: trackers are not set up to surface the right signals, the governance process for routing intelligence does not exist, or Gong is disconnected from the GTM Infrastructure that would let the data change anything. The Diagnosis identifies which one and blueprints the fix.
How exactly does Gong connect to the GTM Infrastructure?
Gong produces conversation intelligence — competitor mentions, objection patterns, stage behaviors, persona signals, champion identification markers. RevShoppe classifies that data and routes it into the Context Layer: the structured foundation that AI reads when generating GTM Workflows. A competitor that comes up on 30% of calls ends up in the battle card. An objection pattern that kills deals in Stage 3 ends up in the rep enablement curriculum. A buying signal that correlates with fast closes ends up as a trigger definition for the next outbound workflow. Gong is the source. The Context Layer is where it becomes actionable.
Does RevShoppe also configure the Gong forecasting module?
Yes. Gong's forecasting module is one of the highest-value features in the platform and one of the most under-configured. RevShoppe sets up deal stages, risk signal definitions, forecast categories, and the CRO review cadence so that pipeline reviews are anchored to conversation signals — not rep-updated probability fields that bear no relationship to what is actually happening in deals.
We use Gong alongside Outreach (or Salesloft). Can you work across both?
Yes, and this is where the integration produces the most value. Gong captures what buyers say. Outreach or Salesloft is where the next message is sent. RevShoppe builds the workflow that connects them: call intelligence from Gong informs the sequence strategy and AI-generated messaging in Outreach or Salesloft, and the outcome of that sequence feeds back into Gong's deal intelligence. The two platforms are not separate deployments — they are two nodes in the same GTM Workflow.
