Your Revenue Organization

Your sellers and agents. Same motion. Different layers.

Your revenue org is no longer just sellers. Agents run the workflows that support them. The same motion — different execution layers, different automation levels.

Your rOS defines which workflows run fully automated, which require human direction, and which require human review before they close. Every agent in your revenue motion gets governed context — not just access.

Human-Led

Deal risk scoring

Hybrid

Post-call follow-up

Deal risk scoring

Agent-Led

Agent executes autonomously. Human governs the context.

Post-call follow-up
Agent logs the call, updates fields, sends the follow-up under policy.

Agent re-scores pipeline continuously, routes at-risk deals to the right owner.

How Your rOS Governs

Every workflow needs governed context. Your rOS provides it.

Your sellers run on process. Your agents run on context. Your RevOps runs on data. All of it runs on the same three systems — and your rOS is the infrastructure that governs what context each one gets.

01
Center of Excellence
Governance

Charter. Decision rights. Policy. Who defines the motion, who can change it, and what context each role is authorized to operate on.

02
Rhythm of Revenue
Cadence

The operating calendar. When your org reviews itself, recalibrates context, and decides what changes — for workflows and the agents running them.

03
Workflow Foundation
Execution

Every motion — discovery, deal cycle, handoff, renewal — runs as a structured workflow. Agent-led or human-led, each one needs the same governed context to execute correctly.

Without a governing substrate, you don't have a hybrid revenue org. You have agents running on assumptions and sellers running on memory — uncoordinated.
