Common Questions

What people ask before reaching out.

What do you need to get started?
▼
Access to your CRM, your GTM tech stack, and a few people who can speak to both how the system was designed and how it's actually being used day to day. Understanding the design intent matters as much as understanding current reality. The gap between the two is usually where the problem lives.
How long does a typical engagement run?
▼
The GTM Diagnosis typically runs six weeks. Larger organizations with more complex systems, more stakeholders, and more red tape take longer. We shoot for six weeks and don't rush a diagnosis to hit an arbitrary deadline.
How much does a typical engagement cost?
▼
RevShoppe engagements are typically six figures, and can reach seven figures for larger or more complex organizations. Every engagement starts with the GTM Diagnosis, which determines what work needs to be done and at what scope. Workflows, system builds, and operating model work are scoped from there.
What does RevShoppe actually deliver?
▼
We design, build, validate, scale, and manage the system until you can manage it on your own. Not a deck. Not a set of recommendations. A functioning commercial infrastructure that runs. We stay until it does.
What's the difference between Catalist Signals and the GTM Diagnosis?
▼
Catalist Signals tells you who's ready to buy and why, based on real-world events rather than intent clicks. The GTM Diagnosis tells you whether your system can act on that signal when it arrives. Most revenue teams need both. The free Conversation Catalist Report is a good place to start if you're early in the conversation.
What types of organizations do you work with?
▼
RevShoppe works with B2B organizations globally, typically Series C through publicly traded. We work across enterprise technology, financial services, manufacturing, and professional services. The GTM execution problems we solve are structural, not vertical-specific or geography-specific.
