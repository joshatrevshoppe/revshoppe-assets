01 — Positioning

What RevShoppe is.

RevShoppe designs, builds, and operationalizes commercial infrastructure for enterprise revenue organizations. We close the revenue execution gap — the space between a company's growth target and the commercial model it's actually running. Our work spans GTM strategy, engineering, and governance: we diagnose gaps across four pillars (Context, Data, People, Technology), design the operating model, build the system, and govern it over time. We don't advise and leave. We build the system and stay until it works.

Who We Sell To

CROs, VP Sales, RevOps leaders, and CCOs at enterprise companies (typically 500–100,000-person orgs). Data-literate. Skeptical of consulting language. Have seen a hundred bad agency decks.

What Makes Us Different

Most consultancies produce slides and leave. We design, build, validate, and scale the system with the team until it runs without us. Our methodology is Design → Build → Validate → Scale (DBVS). Discovery happens inside the Diagnosis phase.

02 — Color System

Six tokens.
One warm color.

The entire palette is six tokens. Coral (#E8634A) is the only warm/chromatic color — everything else is neutral. Never introduce new colors. Never use pure #000 or pure #fff as backgrounds.

Grid Texture — Required on Every Section

Every section has a subtle 1px grid overlay. Dark sections: rgba(255,255,255,0.034) at 40px. Light sections: rgba(0,0,0,0.027) at 56px. Coral CTA sections are the only sections with NO texture — solid coral only.

Type Do's

  • RocWideMedium for all display and H1/H2/H3
  • Montserrat for all body text
  • IBM Plex Mono for eyebrows, labels, buttons, stats — uppercase with 0.08–0.14em tracking
  • RocWideLight Italic only for pull quotes on dark backgrounds
  • Use clamp() for responsive display sizing
  • Letter-spacing -0.01em on large display text

Type Don'ts

  • Never use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or system fonts
  • Never use Space Grotesk or other generic "AI design" fonts
  • Never put body copy in RocWide — it's display only
  • Never use IBM Plex Mono for paragraphs — it's utility only
  • Never use RocWideLight outside of pull-quote contexts
  • Never mix more than these four fonts

05 — Voice & Copy

Specific, active, no hedging.

Our reader is a CRO or VP Sales at an enterprise company. They have seen a hundred bad agency decks and they are skeptical of consulting language. Earn their attention in the first sentence by leading with a number, a named behavior, or a specific consequence.

Headline Patterns That Work
Problem-First

"You are not running outbound. You are running an extremely expensive database management exercise."

Data-First

"$0.007 in pipeline per activity. The benchmark is $0.70–$1.05."

Consequence-First

"Every metric above is recoverable. None of it requires new headcount."

Contrast

"Signals tell you who clicked. Catalist tells you who's ready and why."

Voice Do's

  • Lead with stats, numbers, and named behaviors before explanations
  • Short sentences. Active voice. Present tense.
  • Every claim grounded in real data (see engagement data below)
  • Name the behavior, then the cost, then the fix
  • Anonymize clients — "Series C fintech, 40-person org"
  • Always close references with "Identity confidential"

Voice Don'ts — Banned Language

  • "drive alignment," "unlock value," "best-in-class"
  • "holistic approach," "synergies," "leverage"
  • "actionable insights," "robust," "scalable solutions"
  • "world-class," "next-generation," "transformative"
  • Hedging: "may," "could potentially," "often," "tends to"
  • Fabricated urgency or scarcity of any kind
  • Meaningless CTAs like "See if you qualify"
  • Bullet points for key claims — use stat cards instead

07 — Global Don't List

The lines we don't cross.

Design Don'ts

  • No pure #000 or pure #fff as backgrounds — always use #26242F or #FAFAF9
  • No new colors outside the six-token palette
  • No sections without the grid texture (except coral CTAs)
  • No two same-tone sections back-to-back (dark/dark or light/light)
  • No fonts outside the four approved (Roc Wide, IBM Plex Mono, Montserrat)
  • No generic AI aesthetics — no purple gradients, no Space Grotesk, no stock Tailwind defaults
  • No bullet points for key claims — use stat cards, outcome cards, or numbered steps

Copy Don'ts

  • Never misspell CATALIST, RevShoppe, or RevenueOS
  • Never name clients — always anonymize as "Series X vertical · N-person org"
  • Never fabricate stats — use only the real engagement data above
  • Never use banned consulting language (see full list in voice section)
  • Never say "NDA applies" in Intelligence product copy
  • Never say "you own it completely" in product copy
  • Never repurpose the homepage hero positioning on service pages
  • Never hardcode workflow or metric counts that change
