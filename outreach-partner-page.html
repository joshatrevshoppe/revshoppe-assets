Why it breaks

Outreach is the delivery mechanism. The rest is what makes it work.

Outreach executes the commercial motion. It sends the sequence, logs the call, updates the task. What it cannot do is define who to reach out to, when the trigger is right, what the rep needs to say, or whether the signal was worth acting on. Those are GTM Infrastructure problems — and they live in the three pillars Outreach doesn't touch: Context, Data, and Process. An Outreach implementation without those three pillars doesn't compound. It runs the same broken motion faster.

Common questions

What organizations ask before they engage.

We already have Outreach running. Do we need a reimplementation?
Not necessarily. RevShoppe starts with a GTM Diagnosis that assesses your Outreach configuration against the four infrastructure pillars. Some organizations need a full rebuild. Others need one or two pillars fixed before the existing configuration can compound. The Diagnosis tells you which.
What does a GTM Workflow with Outreach actually look like?
A signal fires — an account shows a trigger event (funding, leadership change, competitive movement, intent spike). Context assembles automatically: company news, CRM history, tech stack, ICP fit score. Claude reads that context and generates a sequence. A rep reviews and approves. Outreach sends. The outcome — meeting booked, no response, bounced — feeds back into the context layer so the next run starts sharper. Outreach is the delivery step. RevShoppe builds everything before and after it.
How is this different from Outreach's own professional services?
Outreach's professional services configure the tool. RevShoppe builds the commercial motion the tool runs. That means ICP definition, signal logic, process design, rep training, AI workflow architecture, and ongoing governance — none of which is in scope for a platform implementation engagement.
Do you work with organizations migrating from Salesloft to Outreach?
Yes. RevShoppe manages Outreach migrations including sequence translation, CRM data integrity during transition, team onboarding, and day-one workflow continuity. We also run the GTM Diagnosis during the migration to ensure the new environment is built on the right infrastructure — not a direct copy of what wasn't working in the previous platform.
