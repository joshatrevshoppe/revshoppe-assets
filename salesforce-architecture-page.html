Common Questions

Straight answers.

Why isn't my sales team using Salesforce?+
Because the system isn't built for how they sell. When Salesforce requires more work than it saves, when data can't be trusted, when automations fire at the wrong time — reps find workarounds and stop logging. That's not a training problem. It's an architecture problem. We fix the system first. Adoption follows.
Can you fix a bad Salesforce implementation without starting over?+
Almost always yes. A full reimplementation is rarely necessary and almost always disruptive. We audit what exists, identify what's salvageable, and rebuild only what's creating active damage. Most organizations have more working infrastructure than they think. It just needs to be cleaned, governed, and connected correctly.
How do I fix my Salesforce forecasting and attribution problems?+
Forecasting breaks when stage definitions aren't standardized and required fields aren't enforced. Attribution breaks when lead source, campaign, and sequence data aren't captured correctly at the point of entry. Both are architecture problems. We fix the data model and validation rules first, then build the reporting layer on top of data you can actually trust.
Can you identify where we have revenue leaks in our go-to-market?+
Yes — and that's a core part of what we do. We're in your instance every day reading the same reports your leadership sees. We look across the full funnel from marketing through close through customer success, and we surface where pipeline is leaking, where handoffs are breaking down, where attribution is missing, and where automation failures are creating invisible drag. The monthly findings report is specifically built around this.
How quickly can we get started?+
Sign up and we schedule an onboarding call within 24 business hours. We get access to your instance, run an initial audit, and have findings in front of you before the end of the first week. You know what's broken and what the plan is before month one is done.
Do you do Salesforce implementations from scratch?+
No. We work with companies that already have Salesforce in place — whether it's a failed implementation, an instance nobody manages, or a system that's grown past what the internal team can handle. If you haven't purchased Salesforce yet, do that first and come back when you're ready to build it correctly.
Do you work with companies outside of tech?+
Yes — and it's where we spend most of our time. Insurance agencies, financial services firms, logistics and freight brokerages, healthcare organizations, staffing companies, manufacturing operations. Industries where the commercial model is complex, the CRM is usually a mess, and the internal team doesn't have the Salesforce and RevOps expertise to fix it. If your team sells, quotes, forecasts, or manages accounts in Salesforce, we can help.
