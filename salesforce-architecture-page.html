Common Questions

Straight answers.

Why won't my sales team use Salesforce?+
Because the system isn't built for how they sell. When Salesforce requires more work than it saves, when data is wrong, automations misfire, and the forecast view doesn't match reality, reps build workarounds and stop logging. That's not a training problem. It's an architecture problem. We fix the system first. Adoption follows because the tool finally works the way the team does.
Our Salesforce implementation failed. Can you fix it without starting over?+
Almost always yes. A full reimplementation is rarely necessary and almost always more disruptive than the original problem. We audit what exists, document what's salvageable, and rebuild only what's creating active financial drag. Most organizations have more working infrastructure than they think, it just needs to be cleaned, governed, and properly connected. We deliver a remediation plan before we touch anything.
We use Salesforce Financial Services Cloud. Is your team equipped for that?+
Yes. Financial Services Cloud introduces complexity that standard Salesforce implementations do not: household objects, relationship maps, referral tracking, compliance-driven workflows, and advisor productivity tooling that breaks when data governance fails. We work with insurance agencies, RIAs, wealth management firms, and banking operations where FSC is the commercial system of record. The architecture principles are the same. The object model and regulatory constraints require specific expertise we have.
We run Salesforce Field Service. Can you help with scheduling and dispatch issues?+
Yes. Field Service Lightning creates a set of operational and data problems that compound quickly when the underlying CRM architecture is not sound: scheduling conflicts, work order attribution failures, technician productivity gaps, and mobile app sync issues that cause data loss in the field. We address the CRM foundation first, then work through the FSL configuration layer and dispatcher workflow. If your field ops revenue is leaking because dispatch and billing don't reconcile with the CRM, that's a solvable architecture problem.
Our AMS and Salesforce aren't talking to each other correctly.+
This is one of the most common disconnects we see in associations, nonprofits, and membership-based organizations. When your Association Management System and Salesforce can't sync member records, event registrations, and renewal data cleanly, both systems become partially unreliable. We map the integration, identify the sync failure points, standardize the field mapping and object relationships, and build a governance process that keeps them aligned. Your team should never have to choose which system to trust.
Our CRM and TMS aren't connected. Salesforce doesn't reflect what's happening in the freight operation.+
In logistics and freight brokerage, a disconnected CRM and Transportation Management System means sales reps are quoting without current carrier data, account managers can't see load history, and leadership can't correlate commercial activity with operational performance. We've worked through CRM-TMS integration for brokerages where reconciling lane data, carrier relationships, and customer revenue required a complete rebuild of how the two systems communicated. The commercial model depends on it.
Salesforce and our ERP aren't syncing. Finance and sales are working from different numbers.+
A Salesforce-ERP disconnect: NetSuite, SAP, Dynamics, or another platform, is one of the highest-cost integration failures in a commercial org. When closed-won in Salesforce doesn't reconcile with recognized revenue in the ERP, your CFO can't trust either system. Quota attainment, commission calculations, and revenue forecasting all break. We identify the disconnect, map the correct data flow, and build an integration architecture that keeps both systems accurate. Finance and sales working from the same numbers is not optional. It is the foundation of a functional commercial model.
How do I fix our Salesforce forecasting and attribution?+
Forecasting breaks when stage definitions aren't standardized and required fields aren't enforced. Attribution breaks when lead source, campaign, and sequence data aren't captured at the point of entry. Both are architecture problems, not reporting problems. We rebuild the data model and validation rules first, then construct the reporting layer on data you can actually trust. Your CRO should be able to walk into a board meeting confident in the number.
Can you identify where revenue is leaking in our go-to-market?+
Yes, and this is a core part of what we deliver. We're in your instance every day reading the same reports your leadership sees. We look across the full funnel from marketing through close through retention, and we surface where pipeline is leaking, where handoffs are breaking down, where attribution is missing, and where automation failures are creating invisible financial drag. The monthly findings report is built specifically to answer the question your CRO is asking but can't get a straight answer on.
